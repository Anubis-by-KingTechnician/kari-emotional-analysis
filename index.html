Executive Summary

Executive Summary: Student Feedback Analysis The student feedback reveals a complex mix of reactions to the creativity course, with several dominant themes emerging across various sentiment categories. Instructor Performance and Teaching Style: Students overwhelmingly praised the professor's teaching abilities, particularly highlighting her engagement, enthusiasm, and genuine care for students. The instructor was repeatedly commended for making potentially challenging content accessible and maintaining high levels of student interest. The personal attention and detailed feedback provided were notably appreciated by students. Course Structure and Content: The course's interactive and flexible nature received mixed responses. While many students valued the open-ended approach and freedom to choose topics, others expressed a desire for more structure, particularly regarding grading criteria and assignment guidelines. The conceptual discussions about creativity were well-received, though some questioned the course's place in an MBA program. Learning Environment and Activities: The interactive elements, including discussions and movement-based activities, were generally well-received. However, group work in breakout rooms presented challenges, particularly when some students were less engaged or participated minimally. The course's physical space and logistics (including Canvas organization) were mentioned as areas for potential improvement. Assessment and Feedback: A significant point of concern centered around assessment clarity. Multiple students expressed frustration with unclear rubrics and grading criteria, particularly for presentations. However, the quality and detail of feedback, once received, was highly praised by several students. Course Impact and Outcomes: Several students reported meaningful personal growth, particularly in public speaking skills and creative thinking. The course appeared to challenge some students' comfort zones productively, though a small but vocal minority found the course inappropriate for university-level education. Areas for Improvement: Key suggestions include: - Clearer assessment criteria and rubrics - Better course organization on Canvas - More structured project guidelines - Better management of group work dynamics - Potentially extending the course duration Notable Concerns: Some specific concerns were raised about COVID-19 safety protocols, cold calling in class, and the course's perceived value relative to its cost. These represent minority viewpoints but should be considered in future course iterations. Overall, the feedback suggests a generally positive reception of the course, particularly regarding instructor quality and personal growth opportunities, while highlighting specific areas for structural and organizational improvements.
